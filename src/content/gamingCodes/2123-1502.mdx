<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(2123-1502 &mdash; eShop connection error)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This code indicates that the Nintendo eShop could not be reached due to a connection-related issue.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">2123-1502 appears when the console is unable to establish or maintain a connection with Nintendo eShop services. The issue occurs at the service communication layer rather than at the account or device level. It does not indicate account enforcement or payment confirmation. The error reflects a failure to complete a network request to the eShop. Service availability or connectivity conditions may be involved.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Where users usually see this code</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Nintendo eShop access attempts</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Store browsing screens</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Purchase or download initiation</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Temporary eShop service interruption</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Network communication failure</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Service availability issue</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">eShop access is blocked</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Store content does not load</span></p>
