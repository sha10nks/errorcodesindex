<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SECTION: Healthcare Insurance / Medical Billing</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SYSTEM: Remittance Advice Remark Codes (RARC)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CODE: N30</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(N30 &mdash; Patient responsibility applies)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This code indicates that a portion of the claim amount has been assigned to patient responsibility under the plan&rsquo;s cost-sharing rules.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">N30 is used to clarify that patient responsibility applies to part or all of the adjudicated charge. It does not determine coverage on its own but provides additional context to other adjustment or responsibility codes. The code signals that the payer has completed its portion of adjudication and that remaining amounts fall under patient cost-sharing obligations. It does not specify the type of responsibility, such as deductible or coinsurance. N30 functions as an informational remark rather than a denial reason.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Where users usually see this code</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Electronic remittance advice (ERA)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Explanation of Benefits (EOB) statements</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Claim line remark sections</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Why this code usually appears</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Deductible, coinsurance, or copayment applies</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Coverage rules assign cost-sharing to the patient</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Plan benefit structure requires patient contribution</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Patient responsibility is reflected on billing statements</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The claim is finalized</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">No further payer action is required</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code is NOT</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a denial of coverage</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a billing error</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a contractual adjustment</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Related error codes</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Notes and edge cases</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This remark often appears alongside other adjustment or responsibility codes. It does not replace specific financial responsibility codes but supplements them. The exact amount assigned depends on plan terms.</span></p>
