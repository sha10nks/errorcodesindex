<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SECTION: Healthcare Insurance / Medical Billing</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SYSTEM: Claim Adjustment Reason Codes (CARC)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CODE: CO-45</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(CO-45 &mdash; Charge exceeds fee schedule)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This code indicates that the billed charge exceeds the amount allowed under the payer&rsquo;s fee schedule or contract.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CO-45 reflects a contractual adjustment where the provider&rsquo;s billed amount is higher than the payer&rsquo;s allowed amount. The insurer reduces the payment to align with the negotiated fee schedule. This is a common contractual adjustment and does not represent a denial of the service itself. It also does not indicate that the service was unnecessary or incorrectly coded. The adjustment is based on pre-established reimbursement rates.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Where users usually see this code</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Provider remittance advice</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Patient EOB summaries</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Contractual adjustment reports</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Why this code usually appears</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The billed charge exceeds contracted rates</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The payer applies standard fee schedule limits</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Network agreement pricing adjustments apply</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Payment is issued at the allowed amount</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The adjusted portion is written off per contract</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">No further claim action is required</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code is NOT</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a denial of coverage</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a billing error</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not patient responsibility</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Related error codes</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CO-151 &mdash; Contractual adjustment</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CO-42 &mdash; Charges exceed contract allowance</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">PR-45 &mdash; Patient responsibility (if applicable)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Notes and edge cases</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Out-of-network claims may show different adjustment behavior. Fee schedules can vary by region and payer. Some plans apply multiple adjustments on a single line item.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Optional next actions</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Review payer contract terms</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Confirm network participation status</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Reconcile adjustments in accounting records</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">External help (optional)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Providers may consult billing or contract specialists to interpret recurring fee schedule adjustments.</span></p>
