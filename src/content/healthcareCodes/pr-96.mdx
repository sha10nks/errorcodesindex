<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SECTION: Healthcare Insurance / Medical Billing</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SYSTEM: Patient Responsibility (PR)</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(PR-96 &mdash; Non-covered charge(s))</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This code indicates that specific charges are not covered and are assigned to patient responsibility.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">PR-96 is used when non-covered charges are assigned directly to the patient. The service may be valid but excluded under plan terms. This code clarifies financial responsibility rather than indicating a billing error. Coverage exclusions drive its application.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Where users usually see this code</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Explanation of Benefits</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Patient billing summaries</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Remittance advice</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Why this code usually appears</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Charges are excluded under plan benefits</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Coverage limits apply</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Policy exclusions enforced</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Patient balance includes the non-covered charges</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The claim line is finalized</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">No further payer action occurs</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code is NOT</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a deductible or coinsurance</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a contractual adjustment</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a temporary hold</span></p>
