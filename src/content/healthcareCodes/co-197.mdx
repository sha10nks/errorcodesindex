<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SYSTEM: Claim Adjustment Reason Codes (CARC)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CODE: CO-197</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(CO-197 &mdash; Precertification required)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This code indicates that required precertification or authorization was not obtained before the service was provided.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CO-197 is used when a payer requires advance approval for a service and no valid authorization is on file. This requirement is determined by plan policy. The absence of precertification affects payment eligibility. It does not automatically reflect on service quality or medical necessity.</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">EOB statements</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Remittance advice</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Authorization review notices</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Authorization not obtained in advance</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Authorization expired or invalid</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Incorrect authorization information submitted</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Payment is denied or reduced</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Review or appeal options may exist</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The claim is closed pending action</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code is NOT</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a documentation error</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a duplicate claim</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a contractual adjustment</span></p>
