<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(N240 &mdash; Incomplete claim)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This code indicates that the claim was incomplete and could not be fully processed.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">N240 is used when required claim elements are missing or incomplete. Payers cannot adjudicate incomplete claims. This is an administrative issue rather than a coverage determination. Once completed, the claim may be eligible for reprocessing.</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Claim rejection notices</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Claim processing summaries</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Why this code usually appears</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Missing claim data elements</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Incomplete service or provider information</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Submission errors</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Claim processing stops</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Reprocessing may be required</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Payment is delayed</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code is NOT</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a coverage denial</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not patient responsibility</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a contractual adjustment</span></p>
