<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(N362 &mdash; Claim adjustment due to incorrect billing)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This code indicates that a claim adjustment was made due to incorrect billing information.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">N362 is used to clarify that an adjustment resulted from billing inaccuracies. This remark provides context but does not independently determine coverage. The adjustment may relate to incorrect codes, units, or billing details. It does not necessarily indicate intent or systemic issues. Corrected billing may allow reprocessing.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Where users usually see this code</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Remittance advice remarks</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Claim adjustment summaries</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Billing correction notices</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Why this code usually appears</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Incorrect billing details submitted</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Units or codes did not align with payer rules</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Billing guidelines were not met</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The claim is adjusted or denied</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Reprocessing may be possible</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Payment may be delayed or reduced</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code is NOT</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a coverage exclusion</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a contractual adjustment</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not patient responsibility</span></p>
