<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CODE: CO-29</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(CO-29 &mdash; Time limit expired)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This code indicates that the claim was submitted after the payer&rsquo;s filing deadline.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CO-29 is applied when a claim exceeds the allowed time frame for submission. Filing limits are set by payer policy and contract terms. Once expired, claims are typically not eligible for payment. This is an administrative determination, not a coverage decision. Exceptions are uncommon and payer-specific.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Where users usually see this code</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Remittance advice</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">EOB statements</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Claim denial notices</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Why this code usually appears</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Late claim submission</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Delays in claim processing</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Filing deadline miscalculation</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The claim is denied</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">No payment is issued</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The claim is closed</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code is NOT</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a coding error</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a temporary delay</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a coverage exclusion</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Related error codes</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(CO-29 &mdash; Filing deadline exceeded)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This code indicates that the claim was submitted after the payer&rsquo;s allowed filing time limit.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CO-29 is applied when a claim is received beyond the payer&rsquo;s established timely filing deadline. Filing limits are defined by payer policy or contractual agreement and are enforced during adjudication. This determination is administrative and does not relate to the service itself. It does not imply that the service was unnecessary or incorrectly billed. Once the filing window has closed, reimbursement is typically no longer available.</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Why this code usually appears</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Claim submission occurred after the deadline</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Delays in claim generation or transmission</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Miscalculation of payer filing limits</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The claim is denied</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">No payment is issued</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The claim is closed</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code is NOT</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a coverage exclusion</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a coding format error</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a temporary processing delay</span></p>
