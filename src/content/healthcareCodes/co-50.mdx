<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SECTION: Healthcare Insurance / Medical Billing</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SYSTEM: Claim Adjustment Reason Codes (CARC)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CODE: CO-50</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(CO-50 &mdash; Non-covered services)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This code indicates that the billed service is not covered under the patient&rsquo;s insurance plan benefits.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CO-50 is used when an insurer determines that a specific service, procedure, or supply is excluded from coverage under the terms of the patient&rsquo;s health plan. The denial is based on benefit design rather than billing format or processing errors. It reflects a contractual coverage limitation, not a mistake in claim submission. The presence of this code does not automatically imply the service was unnecessary or improperly provided. It also does not indicate that the claim was incomplete or incorrectly coded.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Where users usually see this code</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Explanation of Benefits (EOB) statements sent to patients</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Electronic remittance advice (ERA) for providers</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Claim status details within insurer portals</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Why this code usually appears</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The service is excluded under the plan&rsquo;s benefit schedule</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The procedure is not covered for the patient&rsquo;s plan type</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Coverage limitations apply due to policy design</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The charge is denied and not reimbursed by the payer</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Patient responsibility may be assigned depending on plan rules</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The claim is closed unless additional coverage applies</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code is NOT</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a billing or coding error by the provider</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a temporary processing delay</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not an indication of fraud or improper care</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Related error codes</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Notes and edge cases</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Coverage exclusions may vary between employer-sponsored, individual, and government plans. Some plans exclude certain services unless specific conditions are met. Coordination of benefits or secondary coverage may alter final responsibility. Historical coverage changes can also affect how this code appears.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Optional next actions</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Review the plan&rsquo;s summary of benefits</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Confirm whether secondary coverage exists</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Contact the insurer for benefit clarification</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">External help (optional)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">If this code appears repeatedly, some users consult insurance or billing specialists to better understand plan coverage limits.</span></p>
