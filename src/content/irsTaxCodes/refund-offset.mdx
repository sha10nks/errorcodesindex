<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SECTION: IRS / Tax Status &amp; Refund Codes</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SYSTEM: IRS Offset &amp; Adjustment Indicators</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CODE: Refund offset</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(Refund offset &mdash; Refund applied to debt)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This status indicates that all or part of the refund was applied toward an outstanding obligation.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">&ldquo;Refund offset&rdquo; reflects that the refund was reduced to satisfy a qualifying debt. It indicates administrative application of refund funds to another balance. The status does not represent denial but reduction of refund amount. Transcript entries such as code 898 or 820 typically accompany this activity. It signals internal allocation of funds rather than issuance of full refund.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Where users usually see this code</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">On IRS account transcripts</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">In refund status tools after reduced payment</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">In mailed notices explaining offset</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Why this code usually appears</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Outstanding federal or state debt existed</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Administrative offset procedures applied</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Credit balances were used to satisfy liability</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Treasury Offset Program initiated application</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Remaining refund, if any, may be issued</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Account balances update</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Notice entries may appear</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code is NOT</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a freeze</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not an audit</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It does not represent fraud determination</span></p>
