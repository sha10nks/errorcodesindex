<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SECTION: IRS / Tax Status &amp; Refund Codes</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SYSTEM: IRS Account Transcript / Business &amp; Payment Activity</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CODE: 670</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(670 &mdash; Payment received)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This code indicates that a payment has been received and applied to the taxpayer&rsquo;s account.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Code 670 reflects that the IRS has recorded a payment toward the tax account for the specified period. The entry represents receipt and posting of funds, reducing any outstanding balance due. It does not indicate whether the payment fully satisfies the balance or whether additional amounts remain outstanding. The code shows payment activity at the account level but does not specify the payment method within the code itself. It confirms that funds were applied to the account as of the transaction date.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Where users usually see this code</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">On IRS account transcripts under transaction codes</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">After making a tax payment</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">During review of account balance activity</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Why this code usually appears</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">A direct payment was submitted</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">An electronic or mailed payment was processed</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">A prior balance due was partially or fully paid</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">An installment or scheduled payment posted</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The account balance decreases</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Interest calculations may adjust</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Additional payment or adjustment codes may post</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code is NOT</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a refund issuance</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a credit transfer</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It does not confirm full satisfaction of debt</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Notes and edge cases</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Multiple 670 entries may appear if payments are made in installments. The amount posted reflects the payment applied on that date. Reviewing interest and penalty codes provides a complete view of account impact.</span></p>
