<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SECTION: IRS / Tax Status &amp; Refund Codes</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SYSTEM: IRS Notice Classification</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CODE: CP24 notice</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(CP24 notice &mdash; Refund offset notice)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This notice indicates that all or part of the refund was applied to an outstanding obligation.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">A CP24 notice reflects that the IRS offset a refund to satisfy an existing debt. The notice explains the amount applied and the remaining refund, if any. It represents administrative application of funds under offset procedures. The notice does not indicate denial of the entire refund unless fully offset. Transcript entries typically show corresponding offset codes.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Where users usually see this code</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">In mailed IRS correspondence</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">On account transcripts showing offset entries</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">After refund issuance amount differs from expectation</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Why this code usually appears</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Outstanding tax or other qualifying debt existed</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Refund was eligible for offset</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Treasury Offset Program applied funds</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Administrative reconciliation occurred</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Refund amount reflects offset reduction</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Account balances update</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Additional notices may follow if balances remain</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code is NOT</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a refund freeze</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not an audit notice</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It does not indicate fraud</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Notes and edge cases</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Offsets may apply partially or fully to the refund. The notice details the amount applied. Reviewing transcript entries provides confirmation of amounts involved.</span></p>
