<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(IRS account adjustment &mdash; Account balance modified)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This status indicates that the IRS has modified the account balance due to review or correction.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">&ldquo;IRS account adjustment&rdquo; reflects that changes were applied to the account affecting tax, credits, or interest. It signals administrative recalculation within IRS systems. The adjustment may increase or decrease the balance. The status itself does not indicate the reason; transcript entries provide specific transaction codes. It represents account modification activity.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Where users usually see this code</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">On IRS account transcripts</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">After review or amended return processing</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">During refund recalculation</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Correction of reported amounts</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Administrative recalculation required</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Credit or payment reallocation occurred</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Balance may increase or decrease</span></p>
