<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SECTION: IRS / Tax Status &amp; Refund Codes</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">SYSTEM: IRS Notice Classification</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">CODE: CP14 notice</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Title</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">(CP14 notice &mdash; Balance due notice)</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">One-sentence summary</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">This notice indicates that a balance is due on the tax account.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code means</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">A CP14 notice reflects that the IRS has determined an outstanding balance remains unpaid. The notice specifies the amount due, including tax, interest, or penalties if applicable. It represents formal notification of the balance owed. It does not indicate enforcement action at the time issued. The transcript may show assessment and interest entries associated with the balance.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Where users usually see this code</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">In mailed IRS correspondence</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">On account transcripts with balance entries</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">After assessment adjustments</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Why this code usually appears</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Tax liability exceeded payments or credits</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Adjustment increased amount owed</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Payment was not received by due date</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Interest accrued on unpaid balance</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What typically happens next</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Balance remains due until resolved</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Interest may continue to accrue</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Additional notices may follow</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">What this code is NOT</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not a refund notice</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It is not an audit by itself</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">It does not automatically imply enforcement action</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Related error codes</span></p>

<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Notes and edge cases</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">The notice reflects balance at the time issued. Interest may continue to accrue after the notice date. Reviewing transcript entries provides clarity on how balance was calculated.</span></p>
<p class="c1"><span class="c0">Optional next actions</span></p>
