### What should I do when my appliance shows an error code?

First, write down the exact error code displayed. Unplug the appliance for 30 seconds, then plug it back in and try to restart it. If the error persists, consult our error code directory for specific troubleshooting steps. Many codes indicate simple issues you can resolve yourself.

### How do I reset my appliance to clear error codes?

Most appliances can be reset by unplugging them for 30-60 seconds, then plugging them back in. Some models require pressing specific button combinations. Consult your owner's manual for the exact reset procedure for your model. If the error returns, the underlying issue needs to be addressed.

### When should I call a professional for appliance repairs?

Call a professional if the error code indicates electrical issues, motor problems, or control board failures. Also seek professional help if basic troubleshooting doesn't resolve the issue, if you smell burning or hear unusual noises, or if the appliance is under warranty and repairs should be performed by authorized technicians.

### Are appliance error codes universal across brands?

No, error codes are brand and model specific. A code that means one thing on a Samsung washer may mean something completely different on a Whirlpool washer. Always look up codes using your specific brand and model information for accurate troubleshooting.

### What are the most common appliance error codes?

Common codes include water supply issues (4E, F20), drainage problems (5E, F21), door lock errors (dE, F26), and overheating protection (HE, F28). These typically indicate issues with water supply, drainage, door mechanisms, or temperature regulation that can often be resolved with basic troubleshooting.

### Can I prevent appliance error codes from appearing?

Regular maintenance helps prevent many error codes. Clean filters and drain pumps regularly, ensure proper water supply pressure, level appliances properly, avoid overloading, and use appropriate detergents and cleaning products. Follow manufacturer maintenance schedules for optimal performance.

### How do I find my appliance's model number?

Model numbers are typically found on a label or plate inside the door, on the back, or underneath the appliance. Check your owner's manual for the exact location. You'll need the complete model number to look up specific error codes and troubleshooting information.

### What information should I have ready when calling for service?

Have the complete model and serial numbers ready, along with the exact error code displayed. Note when the error occurs (during which cycle or operation), how often it happens, and any unusual sounds, smells, or performance issues you've observed.
