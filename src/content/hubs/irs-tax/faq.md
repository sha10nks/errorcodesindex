### What should I do if I receive an IRS notice?

Read the notice carefully and identify the specific issue. Check the tax year, amount owed (if any), and response deadline. Contact the IRS using the number provided if you need clarification. Respond by the deadline to avoid additional penalties and interest.

### How long does the IRS have to audit my tax return?

Generally, the IRS has three years from the filing date to audit your return. This extends to six years if you substantially understated your income. There's no time limit for audits involving fraud or failure to file a return.

### What is the difference between a CP notice and an LT notice?

CP (Computer Paragraph) notices are automated notices for routine matters like math errors or missing forms. LT (Letter) notices are for more serious issues like audits, collections, or examinations requiring detailed responses.

### How can I check the status of my tax refund?

Use the IRS "Where's My Refund?" tool online or call the IRS refund hotline. You'll need your Social Security number, filing status, and exact refund amount from your return.

### What does Tax Topic 152 mean?

Tax Topic 152 indicates your refund is under review but hasn't been approved yet. This is a normal part of processing and doesn't necessarily mean there's a problem with your return.

### What should I do if I can't pay my tax bill?

Contact the IRS immediately to discuss payment options. You may qualify for an installment agreement, offer in compromise, or temporary delay of collection. Ignoring the bill will result in penalties and interest.

### How do I respond to an IRS audit notice?

Gather all relevant documentation for the items being audited. Consider hiring a tax professional to represent you. Respond by the deadline and provide only the requested information. Keep detailed records of all communications.

### What are my rights during an IRS examination?

You have the right to representation, to appeal disagreements, to request audit reconsideration, and to be treated fairly and professionally. Review IRS Publication 1 (Your Rights as a Taxpayer) for complete information.

### How can I avoid IRS processing delays?

File electronically, double-check all numbers and calculations, include all required forms and schedules, sign and date your return, and ensure your name and Social Security number match IRS records exactly.

### What does it mean when my refund is offset?

A refund offset means all or part of your refund was applied to other debts you owe, such as past-due federal taxes, state taxes, child support, or federal student loans. You'll receive a notice explaining the offset and remaining balance, if any.
