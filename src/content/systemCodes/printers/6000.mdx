<p>SECTION: Systems &amp; Devices</p>
<p>SYSTEM: Printers</p>
<p>CODE: 6000</p>
<p>Title</p>
<p>(6000 &mdash; Paper feed error)</p>
<p>One-sentence summary</p>
<p>The printer reported a paper feed or paper path error during pickup.</p>
<p>What this code means</p>
<p>Error 6000 is commonly used by some printer families to indicate a paper feed issue. It can occur when the pickup rollers cannot feed paper correctly or when paper is detected stuck in the feed path.</p>
<p>Where users usually see this code</p>
<p>Printer panel during paper pickup</p>
<p>After loading unusual paper sizes or media types</p>
<p>Why this code usually appears</p>
<p>Paper jam or skew in the feed path</p>
<p>Dirty or worn pickup rollers</p>
<p>Incorrect paper loading or tray guides misaligned</p>
<p>What typically happens next</p>
<p>Printing stops until the jam/feed issue is cleared</p>
<p>What this code is NOT</p>
<p>It is not an ink/toner low message</p>
<p>It is not a driver installation issue</p>
<p>Troubleshooting checklist</p>
<p>Remove paper and check the feed path for scraps</p>
<p>Reload paper and adjust guides snugly</p>
<p>Clean rollers using manufacturer-recommended method</p>
<p>Try a different tray or different paper type to isolate tray-specific issues</p>
<p>Related error codes</p>
<p>5B00 — Ink absorber/waste ink issue (model-specific)</p>
<p>5100 — Carriage/encoder error</p>
<p>Notes and edge cases</p>
<p>Feed errors can also be caused by humidity, curled media, or overloaded trays.</p>
